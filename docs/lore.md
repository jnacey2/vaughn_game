# Void Dynasty — Lore Bible (MVP)

## Setting

A thousand years ago, the **Sovereign** — a single empire that unified a hundred star
systems — collapsed overnight for reasons no historian agrees on. What's left are the
**Houses**: noble spacefaring dynasties who carved up the wreckage, and the **Drift**, the
derelict hulks of Sovereign megastructures that still leak **Aether**, the only substance
that makes faster-than-light travel possible.

Aether doesn't regenerate. Every generation there is less of it, and every generation the
Houses fight harder over what's left. Great House politics is inseparable from Aether
scarcity: marriages are treaties, children are collateral, and betrayal is a family
tradition. This game's card matches are small skirmishes in that much larger, much older war
— a captain and their fleet, sent to burn an enemy House's ships over a dying resource.

This framing (Aether scarcity, the Drift, the Sovereign ruins) is what a future "objective
track" win condition (contesting a Drift site) would hook into — noted here for continuity
even though it's out of scope for the MVP.

## House Kessler — "Ironbound"

- **Identity**: industrial mining dynasty; slow, tanky, attrition-focused. Their ships are
  ugly, over-armored, and built to survive Aether-mining accidents, not to look good doing
  it.
- **Colors**: rust orange, iron grey, dull brass.
- **Playstyle**: high Hull/Shields, kinetic weapons, few keywords tricks, wins by simply
  outlasting the opponent.
- **Signature Captain: Aldric Kessler** — third son of the Kessler line, never expected to
  inherit, promoted to fleet command after his mother's assassination during a diplomatic
  visit to House Voss space. He is dutiful, humorless, and privately terrified that the
  House is right about who ordered the killing. Passive Trait: friendly units gain +1 Hull
  when deployed. Command Ability: repair a friendly unit's Hull.

## House Voss — "The Unbroken Current"

- **Identity**: a scholar-dynasty that experiments with unstable Aether-reactor tech nobody
  else is willing to risk — faster, more powerful, more dangerous. Their ships hum, crack,
  and occasionally cost them blood to fire.
- **Colors**: violet, cyan, static-white.
- **Playstyle**: aggressive tempo, energy weapons, several cards with the Overload
  keyword — bigger effects at the cost of self-damage.
- **Signature Captain: Mira Kessler-Voss** — daughter of a Kessler-Voss political marriage
  meant to end a decades-old border war (the "Concord Pact"). When her mother, a Voss
  reactor-scientist, was assassinated at a Kessler-hosted summit, the Pact collapsed
  overnight and Mira took her mother's fleet rather than her father's name. She is Aldric's
  half-sister, and she is certain his House ordered the killing he still can't quite believe
  in. Passive Trait: whenever a friendly unit is destroyed, deal 1 damage to the enemy
  Captain (her fleet fights angrier when it's losing). Command Ability: deal direct damage to
  an enemy unit, at the cost of 1 damage to her own Captain (Overload-flavored).

## The Central Drama

Aldric and Mira are half-siblings on opposite sides of the same unsolved murder, each
convinced the other's House is responsible, neither willing to be the one who's wrong. Every
Kessler-vs-Voss match in the game is, narratively, another round of that argument being
settled with ships instead of words. Future content (more Houses, more Captains) should hang
off this same throughline: personal, familial stakes layered on top of the Aether resource
war, not generic "good empire vs. bad empire" conflict.

## Tone guardrails

- Space **opera**, not hard sci-fi — feelings and family politics drive the plot, technology
  is flavor.
- Closer to *Dune*/*Game of Thrones*-in-space than *Star Wars*: no Jedi-equivalent magic
  system, no single chosen-one protagonist. Ships and fleets, not lightsabers.
- Keep flavor text short (one line, in-character) — it should read like a line from a
  character, not a paragraph of exposition.
